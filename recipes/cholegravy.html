Chole Gravy Recipe

Description

Chole bhature Hindi pronuncation: [t͡ʃʰoː.leː bʱə.ʈuː.ɾeː ] is a food dish popular in the northern areas of the Indian subcontinent. It is a combination of chana masala (spicy white chickpeas) and bhatura/puri, a deep-fried bread made from maida.

It is often eaten as a breakfast dish, sometimes accompanied with lassi. It can also be street food or a complete meal and may be accompanied with onions, pickled carrots, green chutney or achaar.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • ⅓ cup sooji
  • ½ tablespoon oil or ghee
  • ½ tablespoon granulated fine sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt or as required
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ cup curd (yogurt)
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons water for kneading or add as needed
  • oil for deep frying

How to Make Chole Bhature Step-By-Step

Here's a brief overview of what you can expect when you make homemade Chole Bhature:

  1. Measure and keep all the ingredients ready for making the gravy.

  2. Heat 2 to 3 tablespoons oil in a pan or kadai/wok.

  3. Add the following whole garam masala (spices):
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 large tej patta (Indian bay leaf)
    • 2 inches cinnamon
    • 2 to 3 cloves
    • 2 green cardamoms
    • 2 to 3 black peppercorns
  4. On low heat fry the whole spices till they are fragrant but don't burn them.

  5. Next add the ground onion-tomato paste.Mix well.

  6. Then add all the dry spice powders
  7. Then add the cooked chickpeas.

  8. Simmer the gravy on a low to medium flame for 12 to 15 minutes or till the gravy thickens.
  9. Serve chickpea curry with onion slices, lemon wedges along with bhatura. Enjoy the curry with bhatura. You can also serve this delicious curry with naan or aloo paratha or plain paratha or kulcha or tandoori roti.
